Introduction & Problem Statement

As global urbanization accelerates, understanding its dynamic impact on microclimates has become a critical challenge for climate scientists and urban planners. The Surface Urban Heat Island (SUHI) phenomenon is highly sensitive to localized surface structures, material configurations, and anthropogenic properties. Traditional land cover classifications are often too broad to capture these structural configurations.

To address this, this project provides an enterprise-grade web application that leverages cloud computing to map and explore **Local Climate Zones (LCZs)** globally. By dividing landscapes into 17 distinctive structural classes based on physical geometry and thermal properties, this system enables user-driven, country-level analysis of urban vulnerability and climate interactions.

Data Source & Academic Reference

The core backend spatial computation integrates the latest global coverage dataset provided by the Bochum Urban Climate Lab. This dataset establishes a comprehensive, high-resolution framework necessary for earth system modeling and local climate investigations.

Official Data Citation:
Demuzere M.; Kittner J.; Martilli A.; Mills, G.; Moede, C.; Stewart, I.D.; van Vliet, J.; Bechtel, B. A global map of local climate zones to support earth system modelling and urban-scale environmental science. Earth System Science Data 2022, 14 Volume 8: 3835-3873. doi:10.5194/essd-14-3835-2022

Technical Methodology & Architecture

Instead of relying on heavy local GIS workflows and massive raster downscaling, the runtime processing pipeline is fully cloud-native, engineered inside the **Google Earth Engine (GEE)** ecosystem:

  • Dynamic Boundary Filtering: The pipeline employs responsive region-of-interest (ROI) masking, allowing users to asynchronously clip global LCZ datasets down to specific administrative national boundaries instantaneously.
  • Statistical Extraction: The backend computes categorical area distributions and zonal summaries across the 17 standardized climate zones to isolate spatial trends in urban development.
  • Client-Side GUI Optimization: Built entirely using the Earth Engine User Interface (`ui.*`) widgets API, implementing adaptive styling frameworks, custom map visualization layers, color tables, and programmatic inspector tools.

Key Research Outcomes & Value

  • On-Demand Global Scalability: Removes computational bottlenecks by enabling planners anywhere in the world to extract granular spatial profiles of their city or nation without needing a desktop GIS configuration.
  • Standardized Urban Comparison: Facilitates direct structural comparisons between cities in different geographic regions, mapping compact high-rises vs. open low-rises alongside natural vegetation and water bodies.
  • Climate-Resilient Infrastructure Design: Provides actionable spatial baselines needed to design local heat mitigation policies, prioritize urban forestry, and optimize land management workflows.
