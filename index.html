About Me

Welcome to my website!

I'm Shacin Chandra Saha, a recent graduate in Geology and Mining from the University of Barishal, currently aspiring to pursue higher education abroad in a Geospatial Science or Earth Observation related program.

My academic journey has combined the study of Earth's physical systems—including Hydrology, Geohazards, Seismology, Mining Geology, GIS, and remote sensing—with practical, hands-on experience. Over four academic years, I participated in geological fieldwork campaigns across various regions of Bangladesh, where we collected geological data, plotted them spatially, and submitted detailed reports based on field interpretation.

Alongside my studies, I actively contributed to several research projects on:

  • Flood hazard zone mapping using Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA)
  • River morphology and coastal erosion monitoring
  • Shoreline dynamics assessment
  • Cyclone vulnerability assessment using FAHP and GIS
  • Urban heat island and land surface temperature analysis

These experiences have strengthened my expertise in ArcGIS Pro, Google Earth Engine, Python, and R, particularly in spatial data analysis, mapping, and geospatial modeling.

I have served as a Trainer in the "GIS and Remote Sensing Basic Course" organized by the Barishal University Science Club in collaboration with GeoAvengers, where I taught nearly 100 students through live sessions, assignments, and assessments. Additionally, I worked as a Teaching Assistant in the "GIS and Remote Sensing: Basic to Advanced Course" funded by the ICT Division of Bangladesh, under the supervision of Professor Md. Abdullah Salman, providing instructional and academic support.

Beyond academics, I've actively volunteered in national-level science and geospatial events, gaining leadership and teamwork experience through my involvement with the Barishal University Science Club and YouthMappers.

Thank you for visiting—I hope this space gives you insight into my passion for geospatial and environmental research.
